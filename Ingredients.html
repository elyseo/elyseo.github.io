Ingredients

Sourdough has four ingredients: flour, water, salt, and time. But not all flour is the same, and that choice shapes everything about the bread you'll bake.

The Most Important Choice

A Guide to Flour

Flour is where most bakers should spend their attention. Protein content, extraction rate, and grain type all affect fermentation, flavour, and crumb structure.

Flour types grouped by category. Higher protein = stronger gluten network = more rise potential.
Category Flour Type Protein % Flavour Best Used For My Preference
White Flour All-Purpose 10–12% Mild, neutral Softer breads, focaccia, cookies ⭐ Yes — my main flour
Bread Flour 12–14% Mild, slightly sweet Country loaves, baguettes, rolls Yes!
High-Gluten / Strong 14–16% Neutral Bagels, very high hydration loaves Occasionally
Whole Grain Whole Wheat 12–14% Nutty, earthy Blend into sourdough (10–30%), sandwich loaves ⭐ Yes — add 20%
Rye Flour 8–13% Complex, slightly sour Dark rye loaves, pumpernickel, starter feed Rarely
Einkorn / Spelt 10–15% Sweet, nutty Heritage grain loaves, experimental bakes Sometimes
Alternative Oat Flour ~11% Mild, slightly oaty Add-ins, quick breads, flatbreads Rarely
Semolina / Durum ~14% Rich, yellow, wheaty Focaccia, pasta breads, ciabatta Rarely
The Pantry

What I Keep Stocked

A baker's pantry doesn't need to be huge — but it should be well-chosen. Here's what I always have on hand.

  • Flours
    • Costco All Purpose Flour (for basic sourdough)
    • King Arthur Bread Flour (high protein, consistent)
    • Bob's Red Mill Whole Wheat (stone ground)
    • Bob's Red Mill Dark Rye (for starter and rye loaves)
    • Semolina (for dusting bannetons and focaccia)
  • Salt
    • Fine sea salt (for dough — dissolves evenly)
    • Maldon flaky salt (finishing — focaccia, pretzels)
  • Fats & Enrichments
    • Extra virgin olive oil (focaccia, flatbreads)
    • Unsalted butter (enriched loaves, brioche)
    • Whole milk (sandwich loaves, rolls)
    • Eggs (enriched doughs)
  • Add-Ins & Flavours
    • Fresh rosemary and thyme (focaccia)
    • Cinnamon and brown-sugar (focaccia)
    • Caraway seeds (rye loaves)
    • Pecans and dried cranberries (fruit & nut loaf)
    • Black sesame and nigella (seeded crust)
    • Cheddar and jalepeno (sourdough)
  • Tools
    • Dutch oven / combo cooker (essential)
    • Banneton / proofing baskets
    • Bench scraper
    • Bread lame (scoring blade)
    • Digital kitchen scale (grams only)
    • Instant-read thermometer
The Other Ingredients

Water, Salt & Everything Else

💧

Water

Use filtered or room-temperature tap water. Heavily chlorinated water can inhibit starter activity — if your starter is sluggish, try filtered water for a week.

Temperature matters: warm water speeds fermentation; cold water slows it. I use 75–78°F water in my dough.

🧂

Salt

Salt strengthens gluten, controls fermentation speed, and — most importantly — makes bread taste like bread. I use 2% salt (relative to flour weight).

Never add salt directly on top of your starter — it can inhibit the yeast. Mix it into the dough separately.

🌱

The Starter

Your starter is the only leavening agent in sourdough. It replaces commercial yeast entirely and adds flavour complexity that yeast bread simply can't match.

A healthy starter used at peak activity (not before, not after) is the single biggest factor in a successful bake.

📝

Ingredient Notes

I love using bobs red mill. But I have recently started using costco's all purpose flour, and I'm loving it!
